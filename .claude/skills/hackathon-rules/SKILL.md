# Hackathon Rules: HackUSF 2026

## Event Overview

- **Event:** HackUSF 2026
- **Organizer:** University of South Florida (MLH-affiliated)
- **Dates:** March 28, 11:30 AM EST – March 29, 11:30 AM EST, 2026
- **Duration:** 24 hours
- **Location:** University of South Florida, Tampa, FL (in-person)

## Challenge Tracks

### General Prize Tracks ($6,000 total pool)
All teams automatically considered. 7 prize categories:
- Best Overall 1st ($1,400 + iPad)
- Best Overall 2nd ($750 + TV)
- Best Overall 3rd ($350 + headphones)
- Most Innovative Hack ($350 + keyboard)
- Best AI Hack ($400 + drone)
- Best Design Hack ($350 + Instax camera)
- Best Beginner Hack ($250 + laptop stand/dock) — requires 50%+ first-time hackers

### Google Cloud: "Building a Self-Healing World with Google ADK"
- **Prize:** $1,750 cash + $50 GCP credits per team
- **Required Tech:** Google Agent Development Kit (ADK), Agent-to-Agent (A2A) protocol
- **Focus Areas:** Climate, Equity, Health, or Disaster Response
- **Objective:** Multi-agent ecosystems solving "Wicked Problems" with measurable social impact

### Oracle: "Technology in Service of Humanity"
- **Prize:** $400 cash + LEGO Super Mario Gameboy + Red Bull Racing merch
- **Focus:** AI/ML solutions addressing human connection gaps
- **Problem Areas:** Loneliness, mental health, accessibility, systemic exclusion
- **Evaluation:** Impact potential, technical execution, vision clarity

## Scoring Rubric

| Criterion | Weight | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Originality/Innovation | 20% | Risk-taking, exploring new tools, building something novel |
| Expertise | 20% | Depth of technical understanding regarding the work |
| Functionality | 20% | Solid, well-executed MVP that's stable and reliable |
| Design | 20% | Aesthetics and user experience |
| Technical Learning | 20% | Explored new technologies, learned new things |

## Timeline

| Milestone | Time |
|-----------|------|
| Hacking begins | Sat Mar 28, 11:30 AM EST |
| Expo-style judging | Sun Mar 29, morning |
| Code freeze | Sun Mar 29, 11:30 AM EST |
| Submission deadline | Sun Mar 29, 12:30 PM EST |
| Closing ceremony + winners | Sun Mar 29, afternoon |

## Demo Format

- **Expo-style:** Live demo at tables, judges rotate between teams
- **Video:** 1-2 minute demo video required (YouTube or Loom)
- Both live expo and video are required for submission

## Submission Requirements

- Project description on Devpost
- Demo video (1-2 minutes, YouTube or Loom)
- Public GitHub repository
- All team members listed on Devpost
- Opt in to sponsor challenges during Devpost submission

## Resources Provided

- $50 GCP credits (for Google Cloud track)
- Workshops during the event
- Mentor support
- Sponsor networking opportunities
- Reliable WiFi at venue

## Constraints & Rules

- All development must occur during the 24-hour hackathon window
- No code or project changes after 11:30 AM Sun code freeze
- Team size: up to 4 members
- Must be 18+ and university students
- Must follow MLH Code of Conduct
- Submissions on Devpost platform
